Asian inspired bbq dish!

Back to previous page

Growing up, this is what my mom always cooks for me and my brother whenever we come home from a sports tournament. We would always eat this with white rice with a soda on the side! Flavored with soy sauce, 5 chinese spices, ginger, and a secret ingredient, this dish is easy and cheap for you college kids! It's perfect when there is an upcomming BBQ! You can either use a charcoal grill to give it a crispy sweet smokey flavor or just pop it in the airfryer for a quick and easy meal!

This sauce works well with boneless chicken thighs, pork belly, pork ribs, and beef. You can use chicken breast if you would like but I reccomend chicken thighs because of how juicy and flavorful than boneless chicken breast. But for this dish, we will be using boneless chicken thighs.

Ingredients

  • 3 pieces of Chicken breast (Boneless or not)
  • 1 tbs of either siracha or Gochujang sauce (if you like it to be sweeter)
  • 2 tsp Soysauce
  • 3 tbs of Garlic powder (you can add more if you want)
  • 1 tbs of Minced Garlic
  • 1 tbs of salt
  • 3 tbs of Oyster sauce
  • 1 tbs of Honey or 4 tbs of Sugar (you can add more)
  • 1 tbs of paprika
  • 2tbs of Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Wash the chicken and pat down the excess water with a paper towel.
  2. Set the chicken on a clean surface (cutting board) and pluck the leftover feathers.
  3. Put the chicken in a large bowl (mixing bowl preferbly).
  4. Dump all the ingredients except for the honey/sugar.
  5. Mix well.
  6. Marinate at least 30 minutes before cooking! (What I like to do is cut off a piece of marinated meat and cook it to see if the seasoning is enough).

After the marinating process is done, time to start cooking! I will be teaching you guys how to cook with the air fryer!

  1. Place one or two chicken pieces (skin side up) in the airfryer at 400 degrees farenheit for 10 minutes.
  2. After the timer goes off, flip the peices and put it in for another 5 to 10 minutes, check regulary.
  3. While that is cooking, pour the honey/sugar into a seperate bowl and pour in a tiny bit of hot water. Stir until the honey/sugar is dissolved.
  4. When the timer goes off, flip the chicken pieces once more(skin side should be facing up) and brush the honey/sugar water on the skin.
  5. Put it in the airfryer for another 2-3 minutes until the skin looks brown and crunchy
  6. Serve with rice or ramen!

If you are using an oven instead, preheat the oven to 400 degrees farenheit for 15-20 minutes each side.
