How to make delicious sweet musubi!

Ever wanted to make spam taste less salty? Or actually, better?This dish originated from Hawaii from a person name Barbara Funamura, a Japanese-American women! This dish is basically grilled spam on top of a block of rice with nori wrapped around it! It's sweet, salty, sticky, and very cheap and easy to mmake!

Ingredients

  • 1 can of spam
  • Some Brown sugar
  • 3 cups of Jasmine Rice
  • 1 bag of Nori Wraps
  • (optional)Either siracha or Gochujang sauce (if you like it to be sweeter)

Steps!

  1. Slice the spam into about 8-10 pieces or depending on how thick you like it (keep the can!). I like mines a little crunchy so I cut them into thin slices.
  2. Grab a pan and heat it until it is hot
  3. Coat the pan with some oil or butter
  4. After the pan is hot and has oil/butter on it, start cooking the spam pieces on medium heat
  5. Put however much siracha/gochujang sauce on it and spread it evenly.
  6. Sprinkle in however much brown sugar you like (more sugar if you want more sweetness)
  7. After 5 minutes flip the spam to cook on the other side. The side that was cooking should look a bit brown and if it is not, keep cooking.
  8. Once you flip the spam, repeat steps 5 to 7
  9. After the spam is done, place them on a plate and set aside.
  10. Grab the Nori and cut them into 3-4 pieces, depending on if you like the Nori thick or not
  11. Grab the spam can and plastic wraps
  12. Put the plastic wrap in the spam can. Use a decent amount of plastic wrap. This is to form and wrap the musubi.
  13. Place one strip of nori at the very bottom of the can and plastic wrap
  14. Put a piece of spam at the bottom, on top of the nori
  15. Then put however much rice you want on top
  16. Press down, but too hard. You want the spam and rice to not fall out once you are snacking on it
  17. After the rice has been placed, fold the Nori on top of the rice
  18. Then grab the plastic wrap sides and pull everything out
  19. Wrap the plastic wraps around the musubi and then you are done!
